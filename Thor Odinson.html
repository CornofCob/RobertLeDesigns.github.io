Thor

Thor Odinson

It’s obvious that when designing the God of Thunder my mind would go to Viking style armor, or at least how Viking looks are often depicted in pop culture. While there is no evidence on whether the Norse warriors wore winged helmets, comic Thor definitely does, and this is something that the movies almost abandoned completely. I think that up to execution, a helmet like this can make or break the design, and I think I settled upon something I’m really pleased with here.

On top of his cape, attached to the two top bolts, is some dark red fur, used here to accentuate the aesthetic and is also a reference to The Mighty Thor: Lord of Asgard comics. The suit that Odinson wears here is a gambeson, with shoulder pads sewn onto to give the god even more stature.

He has wrist guards and leg guards, but the former is held together by strips of animal fur, which is how I interpret the black and yellow tiger pattern on his boots in the classic version. His belt, Megingjörð, in the comic has a big “T” on it with wings on the sides, so I turned that into a somewhat Nordic pattern (now it just looks like a big “M”).

Mjölnir here stays largely the same, albeit a little bit chipped off and weathered. You know, ‘cause all the lightning storms.
